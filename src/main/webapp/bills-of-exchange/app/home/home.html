Description

This dApp (decentralized application) makes possible to create and to transfer electronic bills of exchange using modern distributed ledger technologies (blockchain).

Electronic bills of exchange can be created as smart contracts implementing all all the requirements of an 'instrument' as defined in Geneva Convention or of an 'order in writing' as defined in U.K. Bills of Exchange Act 1882 or of a 'draft' as defined in U.S. Uniform Commercial Code (§ 3-104. Negotiable instrument).

Bills of exchange smart contracts are created by the 'Bills of Exchange Factory' smart contract on 'Create bills of exchange' tab. Every new created smart contract with bills of exchange get its number, Ethereum address and code automatically verified on etherscan.io

Bills of exchange are created as tokens ( ERC20) in smart contract, where every tokens is a bill of exchange in blank - payable to bearer (bearer is the owner of the Ethereum address witch holds the tokens, or the person he/she represents), but not to order - that means no endorsement is possible and the token holder can only transfer the token (bill of exchange in blank) itself.

Using electronic bills of exhange we can easily draw million bills of one dollar instead of a one bill of million dollars. It makes the use of bills more convenient.

Also interesting is the use of electronic bills of exchange in letters of credit (L/C) operations. UCP600 defines that one of the form of L/C is where the issuing bank has "to accept a bill of exchange (“ draft”) drawn by the beneficiary and pay at maturity if the credit is available by acceptance" (see: International Chamber of Commerce. UCP 600: Uniform Customs and Practice for Documentary Credits)

The fact that bills of exchange are technically realized in the form of tokens makes it possible to use them in other smart contracts. For example, for using escrow for receiving payments on bills of exchange, or for automatic payments on bills through smart contracts, the latter is especially interesting when using stablecoins convertible to fiat such as xEUR.

To create or accept bills of exchange via our 'Bills of Exchange Factory' a person has to use Ethereum address verified on Cryptonomica (for TestNet we use a mock-up smart contract that returns a mock-up personal data for every Ethereum address), and has to sign an arbitration agreement that makes possible to make legal claims on payments on bills and to resolve disputes in our online arbitration tribunal. See more on our approach to private law in Cryptonomica: ex aequo et bono and new lex mercatoria .

See also a page on the Cryptonomica wiki about our electronic bills of exchange

Description and legal part:

A bill of exchange (also known as a 'draft') is a written order by the drawer to the drawee to pay money to the payee.

A common type of bill of exchange is the cheque (check in American English), defined as a bill of exchange drawn on a banker and payable on demand.

If the drawer and drawee is the same person, bill of exhange can be can be considered the same as promissory note.

Bill of exhange is an ancient financial instrument which is already legal and regulated in most countries. The concept of a bill of exchange is about the same in all countries, what causes their frequent use in international trade and finance.

Bill of exchange has long been considered as de-facto form of legal private money, and blockchain based bill of exchange can be a perfect instrument for creation of stablecoins.
